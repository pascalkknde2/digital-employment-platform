# 🏆 DIGITAL EMPLOYMENT SUPPORT

### Winning Bid Proposal Structure

*(Aligned to Kirklees Council requirements)*

---

# 1. 🔷 Executive Summary (Score Booster Section)

**Purpose:** Convince evaluators in 1–2 pages

### What to include:

* Clear understanding of the challenge:

  * Digital exclusion
  * Economic inactivity
  * Neurodiversity inclusion gap

* Your solution in one sentence:

  > “We deliver a data-driven, inclusive digital skills programme that transitions participants from exclusion to employment.”

* Key outcomes:

  * % participants gaining digital confidence
  * % progressing to employment or training
  * Employer engagement (Lot 2)

👉 **Tip:** Use **numbers + outcomes**, not descriptions.

---

# 2. 🎯 Understanding of Requirements

Break it down per LOT.

---

## LOT 1a & 1b – Inactive Participants

### Show you understand:

* Barriers:

  * Low confidence
  * No devices / internet
  * Fear of technology
* Needs:

  * Simple, guided learning
  * Practical outcomes (email, CV, job search)

---

## LOT 2 – Neurodiversity

### Show deeper insight:

* Workplace barriers:

  * Communication challenges
  * Sensory overload
  * Lack of employer awareness

👉 Position yourself as:

> “Not just trainers, but **inclusion specialists**”

---

# 3. 🧩 Service Delivery Model (CORE SECTION)

This is where bids are won or lost.

---

## 🔹 3.1 Participant Journey (VERY IMPORTANT)

Design a clear journey:

1. **Outreach & onboarding**
2. **Digital skills assessment**
3. **Personalised learning plan**
4. **Training (up to 40 hours)**
5. **Milestone achievements**
6. **Employment pathway support**
7. **Post-programme follow-up**

👉 Add a simple diagram (if submitting PDF)

---

## 🔹 3.2 Training Delivery

### Format:

* Blended learning:

  * In-person workshops
  * Online sessions
  * Self-paced modules

### Content:

* Email, Word, Excel
* Teams / Zoom
* Online safety
* Job search tools

👉 Add:

* Real-life tasks (e.g., “apply for a job online”)

---

## 🔹 3.3 Digital Inclusion Support

Strong bids include:

* Device access support
* Connectivity guidance
* 1:1 mentoring

---

## 🔹 3.4 LOT 2 – Neurodiversity Delivery

### Employees:

* Assistive tech tools
* Productivity strategies
* Digital workflows

### Employers:

* Training workshops
* Inclusion toolkits
* Manager coaching

👉 Add:

* Digital resources (videos, podcasts, guides)

---

# 4. 💡 Innovation & Added Value (DIFFERENTIATOR)

This is where you outperform competitors.

### Examples:

* AI-based skill assessment
* Gamified learning platform
* Job-matching dashboard
* Digital badges / certifications

👉 Even simple innovation scores highly if:

* Practical
* Scalable
* Measurable

---

# 5. 📊 Outcomes & KPIs

Define measurable success.

### Core KPIs:

* # participants enrolled
* % completing programme
* % gaining digital skills
* % entering employment/training
* Employer satisfaction (Lot 2)

### Example:

> “80% of participants will create and use an email independently within 2 weeks.”

---

# 6. 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Staffing & Expertise

### Include:

* Trainers (digital skills)
* Employment advisors
* Inclusion specialists (for Lot 2)

### Add:

* Experience with:

  * Vulnerable groups
  * Community delivery
  * Neurodiversity

👉 Use real profiles (even short bios)

---

# 7. 🤝 Partnerships & Local Engagement

This is critical for councils.

### Show:

* Local partnerships:

  * Job centres
  * Community groups
  * Employers

👉 Strong message:

> “We are embedded in the Kirklees ecosystem”

---

# 8. 🛠️ Technology & Tools

### Include:

* Learning platform (if any)
* Communication tools
* Progress tracking dashboards

👉 Keep it simple:
Councils prefer **usable**, not overly complex systems.

---

# 9. 📅 Implementation Plan

### Timeline:

* Month 1: Setup & recruitment
* Months 2–10: Delivery
* Months 11–12: Outcomes & reporting

### Include:

* Milestones
* Delivery phases

---

# 10. ⚠️ Risk Management

### Identify risks:

* Low engagement
* Dropouts
* Digital access issues

### Provide mitigation:

* Flexible delivery
* 1:1 support
* Incentives

---

# 11. 💷 Value for Money

### Show:

* Cost per participant
* Efficiency
* High impact per £ spent

👉 Example:

> “£X per participant for 40 hours of training + employment support”

---

# 12. 📈 Monitoring & Reporting

### Include:

* Weekly tracking
* Monthly reports
* Final impact report

### Metrics:

* Attendance
* Progress
* Outcomes

---

# 13. 🌍 Social Value (HIGH SCORING IN UK TENDERS)

### Show:

* Local hiring
* Community impact
* Inclusion focus
* Sustainability

👉 This can decide the winner.
